How long should it take to detail a car?

Regular detailing is crucial for maintaining your vehicle’s appearance and value. Whether you’re tackling it yourself or hiring a professional, one common question arises: How long does it take to detail a car? The answer varies depending on several factors. In this post, we’ll break down these factors and give you a better understanding of how long a typical car detailing session should take.

What does car detailing service involve?

Before diving into the time frame, it’s important to understand what car detailing entails. Car detailing is a thorough cleaning and restoration process beyond a basic car wash. It aims to restore your vehicle to its original, like-new condition – inside and out. This process includes intricate cleaning, polishing, and sometimes minor restoration work. Below are the main components of car detailing:

Exterior detailing

  • Washing and drying
  • Using a clay bar to remove surface contaminants
  • Polishing and buffing
  • Applying wax or sealant for protection
  • Cleaning and dressing tires and rims
  • Window and trim cleaning

Interior detailing

  • Vacuuming seats, carpets, and floor mats
  • Shampooing carpets and upholstery
  • Leather cleaning and conditioning
  • Cleaning and polishing the dashboard, console, and trim
  • Window and mirror cleaning
  • Odor removal and sanitization

Some detailers may also offer additional services like engine cleaning, headlight restoration, or paint correction.

How long does each type of detailing take?

The time required to detail a car depends on whether you opt for interior, exterior, or complete detailing. Here’s a general time breakdown:

Interior detailing

Detailing the interior can take two to four hours, depending on the car’s condition. Heavily stained, pet-hair-covered, or odorous vehicles will naturally take longer. For instance:

  • Quick interior cleaning: If your car’s interior is relatively clean, with light dust and minimal dirt, it may take around two hours.
  • Deep cleaning: For vehicles with stubborn stains, spills, or pet hair, expect the process to take three to four hours, as it requires more intensive shampooing, stain removal, and odor treatment.

Exterior detailing

Exterior detailing generally takes between one and a half to three hours, depending on the service level and the condition of the vehicle:

  • Basic exterior wash and wax: A simple exterior wash with a wax finish typically takes one and a half to two hours.
  • Full exterior detail: This service includes washing, clay bar treatment, polishing, and waxing, which can take two to three hours. Polishing alone is time-consuming, as it involves removing paint imperfections.

Full Detailing

A comprehensive detail that includes both interior and exterior work usually takes four to six hours or more. If the vehicle is well-maintained, four hours might be sufficient. However, if it’s heavily soiled or requires extensive work (like scratch removal or odor elimination), expect the job to take six hours or longer.

Factors affecting car detailing time

Now that you know the general timeframes, let’s examine the factors affecting how long it takes to detail a car. These factors can significantly impact the time needed for the job.

Vehicle size

The size of the car is one of the most significant factors:

  • Small cars (sedans, coupes): These smaller vehicles typically take less time to detail due to their limited surface area. A complete detail might take around four to five hours.
  • SUVs, trucks, and vans: Larger vehicles can take six to eight hours or more to detail because they have more surface area to clean, both inside and outside.

Vehicle condition

The current state of your car also plays a crucial role:

  • Well-maintained vehicles: If you clean your car regularly, the detailing process will be faster and may fall on the lower end of the time spectrum.
  • Heavily soiled vehicles: Cars with significant dirt buildup, stains, pet hair, or odor issues will require more time for the detailer to clean and restore them, possibly adding two or more hours to the job.

Type of service requested

The type of detailing service you request also impacts the time:

  • Paint correction: Removing scratches and swirl marks can be a lengthy process, adding several hours to the job.
  • Headlight restoration: Cleaning and restoring cloudy headlights may take 30 minutes to one hour.
  • Odor removal or pet hair treatment: Specialized treatments, such as ozone odor removal or tools for removing pet hair, can extend the time by another hour or more.

Detailer’s experience and equipment

The skill level and equipment of the detailer also make a difference:

  • Experienced detailers: Professionals with experience can complete the job faster without compromising quality. They know the best techniques and have streamlined their processes.
  • Professional-grade equipment: Tools like steam cleaners, polishers, and extractors can cut down on the time needed for detailing while delivering superior results.

How to reduce detailing time

While detailing naturally takes time, there are a few ways you can help reduce the time it takes:

  • Maintain your car regularly: Regular cleaning of your vehicle, both inside and out, prevents dirt buildup, making it quicker for the detailer to restore your car.
  • Declutter before your appointment: Remove any personal belongings, trash, and clutter from your car before taking it in for detailing. This allows the detailer to start working immediately without clearing out the interior.
  • Communicate your needs: Let the detailer know precisely what services you require. If you don’t need deep cleaning or polishing, they can focus on the areas that are most important to you.
